Company Overview

In the early 20th century, Eugène Paul Louis Schueller (1881–1957), a young French chemist, developed a hair dye formula called Oréale. Schueller formulated and manufactured his own products, which he sold to Parisian hairdressers. On 31 July 1909, Schueller registered his company,[7] the Société Française de Teintures Inoffensives pour Cheveux (Safe Hair Dye Company of France). The company eventually became L'Oréal. In 1920, the company employed three chemists; the number of employees continued to grow with 100 by the year 1950, and 1,000 by the year 1984; as recently as 2021, there was an estimated total of 85,252 worldwide.[8] L'Oréal got its start in the hair-color business, but the company soon branched out into other cleansing and beauty products. As of 2020, L'Oréal markets in all sectors of the beauty business: hair color, permanents, hair styling, body and skincare, cleansers, makeup, and fragrance. The company's products are found in a wide variety of outlets, from hair salons and perfumeries to supermarkets, health/beauty outlets, pharmacies, and direct mail.

Global Presence

With a presence in over 150 countries, L'Oréal India contributes to the Group's global mission of beauty for all, offering tailored solutions to diverse beauty needs around the world. Global presence refers to the ability of an individual, organization, or entity to establish a significant footprint across international markets, regions, or platforms. In today’s interconnected world, having a global presence is a hallmark of influence, adaptability, and success. It indicates the ability to transcend geographical boundaries while meeting the needs of diverse cultures and markets.

Sustainability Efforts

Committed to reducing environmental impact, L'Oréal India uses eco-friendly packaging, reduces water usage, and lowers carbon emissions as part of the Group's 'L'Oréal for the Future' sustainability program. Sustainability efforts encompass actions and strategies aimed at meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own. These efforts span environmental, social, and economic domains, emphasizing the balance between human activities and the Earth's ecosystems.
